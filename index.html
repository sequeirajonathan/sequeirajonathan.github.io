Interdependent Ecosystem

The Everglades is a diverse ecosystem of tropical wetlands in south Florida. The systems originate from the Kissimmee River in the central region of Florida. The river system runs off into Lake Okeechobee.This is where the main source of fresh water enters the Everglades. The Everglades is composed of complex ecosystems such as Cypress Swamps, Mangrove Tree Systems, and Hardwood Hammocks. There is also a vast array of weather patterns that range from drought in the dry season December - April and flooding during the wet seasons May - November

Ecosystem in Danger!

This fantastic ecosystem and diverse domain for wildlife is in grave danger. For centuries people have been building architecture such as dams, canals, agricultural development and the construction of homes. Due to the demand of expansion and growth, the Everglades has suffered dramatically, and it's only going to get worse if we don't do something to support conservation effort. The Everglades was once a massive ecosystem but it is slowly depleting, and we must do our best to maintain it.
